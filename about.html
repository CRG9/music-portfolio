My Roadmap to Artistry

1. Guitar Hero

Most families that were into gaming back in the late 2000's will remember
how cool it was to play along to history's greatest rock songs from the
comfort of their living rooms.

For some, this was the spark that led to the urge to pick up a guitar and start jamming for real.

2. TABS

Now grabbing my first guitar after playing some Guitar Hero didn't start with me being able to read music notes like the school band would have taught.

Instead, I did what any eager kid would do when they wanted to learn a cool song without all of the expertise:

I learned how to read Tabs!

3. Powerchords

Now as a novice, playing Tabs got tricky while my desire to jam on my own grew exponentially.

This brought me towards experimenting with Powerchords!

Sure enough, my 5th grade friends and I wrote a song for parent appreciation day, called "Thank You Mom and Dad"Still searching for the video... and performed it for our classmates and our parents. You can bet your ass it had Powerchords leading the tune!

4. Guitar Lessons & The Pentatonic Scale

Eventually I found myself lucky enough to take guitar lessons that my parents were willing to fund for me.

Back in the 5th grade, I believe the arrangement was $150 for four 30 minute lessons in the SunVet Mall Music Store

I ended up getting grounded and didn't take a another guitar lesson after my 4th and final session but that's another story for another time...

I can certainly say that the Pentatonic Scale is the foundation that single-handedly built my ability to solo over the last 15 years!

5. Chords

Once I learned how to piece together chords, I unlocked my ability to put together songs. Chords allow for the base of a song to be made - typically a base that solos and lead guitar can be layered on top of.

My favorite chord is the G Major chord, likely because it was the first one I ever learned.

How beautiful the sound indeed...

6. Soloing

After thousands of hours listening to greatest hits, it became very evident what made songs stand out.

Something about hearing what notes and melodies could fit between two measures became evermore aparent as time went on.

This inevitably strengthened my ability to solo with a pure creative spark and sense of direction!

7. Garageband for iPad

Putting all of these components together inevitably led to working on Garageband.

What better way to make music from the comfort of my bedroom without access to a studio? That's where Garageband for iPad came in.

Over the course of 8 months, I drafted 14 songs that truly sewed the fabric to my committment to music going forward.

8. The Booth

A temple, a sanctuary, a housing for creativity - whatever you call it, it's a pact I made with myself to take the creations of my leisure and evolve them into respectable pieces of art.

After $1,500+ and 80 hours to draft and construct, the booth now stands as my house of music.

Those who have a deep and undying love for the process of creation are welcomed and encouraged to pay it a visit!

My Greatest Influences & Inspirations

I study these artists not because of their range or depth... or their challenge to a status quo.
I study them because they know how to connect with the most ears.

And while I don't take any pride in studying the greatest artists, I do hold with high regard my taste for a variety of genres of music.
For me, this reinforces the overall love and interest in music as a whole, rather than "good" music alone.
